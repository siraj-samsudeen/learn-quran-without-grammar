Memorization screen · three alternate philosophies

The v2 grid you liked is one model. Here are three that solve the problem very differently. They could coexist — different tools for different moments.

QUESTION
Do you know the last 10 short surahs?
That's Al-Fīl (105) through An-Nās (114) — they're the shortest ones at the very end of the Qur'an.

α · Q&A wizard

5–8 yes/no questions. "Juz 30?" · "Al-Fātiḥa?" · "Ayat al-Kursī?" · "Yāsīn?" · "Other surahs? (type to add)." Each yes = bulk-mark. Done in 30 seconds. Kid-friendly. Best fit for AA onboarding moment.

β · Mushaf page-book

Scroll through the actual 604 Qur'an pages. Each page shows its Arabic text; tap to toggle. Matches how traditional ḥifẓ students think ("I know pages 590–604"). Slow, but ritually satisfying. Best for students deep in ḥifẓ.

γ · Audio-assisted listener

App plays ~10 seconds of each surah (short ones first). Three buttons: Know it / Partial / Don't know. Walks through Juz 30 in ~5 min. Kids who memorized by ear may not know surah names but recognize sounds instantly. Shazam-for-ḥifẓ.

MY RECOMMENDATION — THEY COEXIST
α · Wizard powers the AA post-Lesson-1 onboarding (fast, kid-friendly, 30 seconds).
U · v2 Grid is the BB default profile view (precise, editable, visual status at a glance).
β · Mushaf is an optional drill-in view from U ("view as pages" toggle — already in v2). Opt-in for ḥifẓ students.
γ · Audio is a "Double-check my memorization" tool surfaced from U's menu. Runs once or on demand. Self-verify.

Each tool earns its place by being the right shape for a different moment. The data model is the same — studentRecitation rows per surah/verse-range — only the input UX changes.
