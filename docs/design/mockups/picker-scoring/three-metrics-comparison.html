Three Scoring Metrics — Which to Keep?

All use unique lemmas (your fix applied). No metric exceeds 100%.

M1: Avg Frequency

Σ(token freqs) / word count

Answers: "How common are the words?"
Length bias: None (normalized)
Includes: All words incl. function

M2: Total Coverage %

Σ(unique ALL lemma freqs) / total

Answers: "What % of the Qur'an can the student recognize?"
Length bias: Favors longer
Includes: All words incl. function

M3: Content Coverage %

Σ(unique CONTENT lemma freqs) / total

Answers: "How much new vocabulary unlocked?"
Length bias: Favors longer
Includes: Content words only

What the stacked bars show

Each row's bar has two segments: ■ green = function-word coverage and ■ pink = content-word coverage. Together they equal M2 (Total Coverage).

Notice how function words (green) are nearly constant across verses (~15-33%) while content words (pink) vary dramatically. This means M2 is mostly noise from function words + a content signal. M3 isolates just the signal.

But for Lesson 1 (Hanzala's first-ever Arabic exposure), that "noise" IS signal — he doesn't know و or ال yet. By Lesson 5+, function words are fully memorized and M3 becomes the better metric.

Recommendation: Keep all three, weight by lesson number

Early lessons (1-3): M2 (Total Coverage) gets full weight — every word matters, including particles.
Mid lessons (4-7): Blend M2 and M3 — function words partially known, content matters more.
Later lessons (8+): M3 (Content Coverage) dominates — function words are fully known by now.
M1 (Avg Frequency) stays as a length-neutral tiebreaker at every stage — prevents the picker from always favoring long verses.
