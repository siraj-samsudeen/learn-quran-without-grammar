Your Metric 2 (M2): Σ(all word freqs) / total Qur'an words — what % of the Qur'an this verse "covers." ⚠ Can exceed 100% (double-counts repeated lemmas).
Proposed fix — True Coverage: Σ(unique lemma freqs) / total — each lemma counted once, regardless of repetition in the verse. Cannot exceed 100%.

What the numbers reveal

M2 (your Metric 2) breaks on long verses — Ayat al-Kursi scores 177.9%, which is meaningless. This happens because و (freq 9,594) appears 6 times, adding 57,564 to the sum. The student only learns و once.

M1 (your Metric 1) is dominated by function words — 2:163 scores M1 = 2,672 (looks great!) but has only 4 content lemmas. The high average comes from ال, و, لا, إلّا — words the student already knows.

True Coverage (unique lemma) fixes the >100% bug — Ayat al-Kursi = 41.8% (learn 38 unique lemmas → recognize 41.8% of all Qur'an word-tokens). That's a real, meaningful number.

Content Coverage is the purest signal — strips function words, counts unique lemmas once. Ayat al-Kursi = 8.58% (29 content lemmas). This is the real teaching value: "how much new vocabulary does this verse unlock?"

Recommendation: use both

M1 + Content Cov%
M1 (avg freq/word) — quality filter: "are the words worth learning?"
Content Coverage % — value filter: "how much Qur'an does this verse unlock?"

M1 compares verses of any length fairly.
Content Cov% rewards verses that teach more unique content words.
