# Lesson 1 — Selection Log: اللهُ أَكْبَرُ

Anchor phrase: **اللهُ أَكْبَرُ**
Root words: **كَبُرَ** (kabura) · **إِلَٰه** (ilāh)

---

## Root word 1: كَبُرَ (kabura) — root ك ب ر — 161 occurrences, 18 forms

### Why these 5 forms?

| Form | Count | Why selected |
|------|-------|-------------|
| أَكْبَر (akbar) | 24 | The anchor word itself — must include |
| كَبِير (kabīr) | 40 | Most frequent nominal, Name of Allah |
| اسْتَكْبَرَ (istakbara) | 40 | Most frequent verb, central Qur'anic theme (Iblīs, Pharaoh) |
| كَبُرَ (kabura) | 8 | Powerful verses, Form I base verb |
| كُبْرَى (kubrā) | 7 | "Greatest" feminine — vivid imagery (signs, fire, calamity) |

### Why not the others?

| Form | Count | Reason deferred |
|------|-------|----------------|
| كَبَّرَ (kabbir) | 4 | **High priority for revisit** — directly means "do takbīr" |
| مُتَكَبِّر (mutakabbir) | 7 | **High priority** — Name of Allah (59:23) |
| Others (11 forms) | 1–7 each | Lower frequency or niche usage — see pipeline |

## Root word 2: إِلَٰه (ilāh) — root أ ل ه — 2,851 occurrences, 3 forms

### Why pattern-based selection?

This root has only 3 derived forms: إِلَٰه (147), اللَّه (2,699), اللَّهُمَّ (5). Since اللَّه appears 2,699 times across the entire Qur'an, the teaching material comes from إِلَٰه — selected by **sentence pattern** rather than morphological form.

### Why these 5 patterns?

| Pattern | Why selected |
|---------|-------------|
| لَا إِلَٰهَ إِلَّا | The Tawḥīd formula — shahādah, most important pattern in Islam |
| إِلَٰهٌ وَاحِدٌ | Core tawḥīd concept — "One God" |
| آلِهَة | False gods / idols — essential contrast with tawḥīd |
| مِنْ إِلَٰهٍ غَيْرُهُ | Every prophet's repeated call — universal pattern |
| إِلَٰهِ النَّاسِ / اللَّهُمَّ | Prayer forms — daily use, connects to lived practice |

### Why not the others?

| Pattern | Reason deferred |
|---------|----------------|
| إِلَٰهَهُ هَوَاهُ | "Took his desire as his god" (25:43, 45:23) — striking but niche |
| أَإِلَٰهٌ مَعَ اللّٰه | Five rhetorical questions in 27:60–64 — powerful sequence, future candidate |

## Hadith selection

**Selected:** Words Beloved to Allah (Ṣaḥīḥ Muslim) — bridges both roots in one hadith: لَا إِلَٰهَ إِلَّا اللّٰه + اللهُ أَكْبَرُ.

**Deferred to pipeline:** Danger of Kibr (atom's weight), Kibr is Allah's Cloak (Qudsī), Twelve Angels Rushed (gates of heaven), Sins Washed Like Sea Foam.

**Dropped:** Key to Prayer (overlap with anchor), Opening of Prayer / Eid Takbīr / Sleep Dhikr (duplicate content), Umar's Takbīr in Mina (seasonal/niche).
