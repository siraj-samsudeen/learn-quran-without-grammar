## Root word 2: كَبُرَ (kabura) — root ك ب ر

### Table entries (5, centered with transliteration, logically ordered)

| # | Arabic | Transliteration | English | Ordering logic |
|---|--------|----------------|---------|---------------|
| 1 | كَبُرَ | kabura | was great | Base verb |
| 2 | اسْتَكْبَرَ | istakbara | was arrogant | Derived verb — "claimed greatness for oneself" |
| 3 | كَبِير | kabīr | great, big | Adjective (masculine) |
| 4 | كُبْرَى | kubrā | greatest | Adjective (feminine) |
| 5 | أَكْبَرُ | Akbar | greater, greatest | Superlative — lands on the anchor word |

### Anchor phrase

| Ref | Arabic | English | Why chosen |
|-----|--------|---------|-----------|
| — (teaching phrase) | هُوَ كَبِيرٌ. أَنَا أَكْبَرُ مِنْهُ. وَاللَّهُ أَكْبَرُ مِنَّا، بَلْ، مِنْ كُلِّ شَيْءٍ | He is great. I am greater than him. And Allah is greater than us — rather, than everything. | Made-up teaching phrase. Uses 3 forms (كَبِير, أَكْبَر, أَكْبَر) in one flowing sentence. Builds from human comparison to divine, lands on اللهُ أَكْبَرُ. |

### Learning phrases (3)

| # | Ref | Arabic | Form taught | English | Why chosen |
|---|-----|--------|------------|---------|-----------|
| 1 | 79:20 | فَأَرَاهُ الْآيَةَ **الْكُبْرَىٰ** | كُبْرَى | He showed him the greatest sign | Tiny — 4 words. Mūsā showed Pharaoh the greatest miracle. |
| 2 | 61:3 | **كَبُرَ** مَقْتًا عِندَ اللَّهِ أَن تَقُولُوا مَا لَا تَفْعَلُونَ | كَبُرَ | Greatly hateful in the sight of Allah that you say what you do not do | Base verb form. Saying and not doing — deeply hated by Allah. |
