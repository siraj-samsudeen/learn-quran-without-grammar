## Anchor

**اللهُ أَكْبَرُ**

You say this every single day. Before Fajr, before Ẓuhr, before every ṣalāh. You raise your hands and say it. The muezzin opens with it. It is the first thing you hear when prayer is called, and the first thing you say when you stand before Allah.

Today, we open up this one word: **أَكْبَرُ** — and the root underneath it.

---

## Root Hub: ك ب ر

The root **ك ب ر** carries a core meaning: **something is great, large, or grows.**

From this one root, the Qur'an builds many forms — each one a different shade of "greatness." Some describe Allah's majesty. Some describe human arrogance. Some describe old age. Some describe great sins. All come from the same three letters.

**Total Qur'anic occurrences: 161 across 18 forms**

## Qur'anic Ayahs by Family

### بَاب ١ — Base Forms (Form I)

The simplest level of the root: **ك ب ر** as a verb, and the nouns and adjectives built directly from it — no special prefix, no added pattern. This is where the root's basic meaning lives: *something is great, large, old, or heavy.*

### بَاب ٢ — Form II: كَبَّرَ (kabbara) — "to magnify / declare great"

**Pattern: فَعَّلَ — the middle letter is doubled (shadda).**

Form II often means to do something intensely, or to make/declare something to be the root meaning. So if the root ك ب ر means "great," then كَبَّرَ means "to declare something great" — to magnify. This is the origin of the word **تَكْبِير** and the act of saying اللهُ أَكْبَرُ.

**4 verb occurrences + 1 verbal noun (تَكْبِير) in the Qur'an.**

| Ayah | Arabic Fragment | Translation | Note |
|------|----------------|-------------|------|
| **74:3** | وَرَبَّكَ **فَكَبِّرْ** | And your Lord — magnify [Him] | One of the first commands ever revealed |
| **17:111** | وَ**كَبِّرْهُ** **تَكْبِيرًا** | And magnify Him with [great] magnification | The command to make takbīr — verb + verbal noun together |

> Other occurrences: 2:185, 22:37

---

### بَاب ٣ — Form IV: أَكْبَرَ (akbara) — "to find great / be astonished"

**Pattern: أَفْعَلَ — a hamza (أ) is added before the root.**

Form IV can mean to find or consider something to be the root quality. So أَكْبَرَ means "to find something great" — to be struck by its greatness, to be overwhelmed.

**1 occurrence in the Qur'an.**

| Ayah | Arabic Fragment | Translation | Note |
|------|----------------|-------------|------|
| **12:31** | فَلَمَّا رَأَيْنَهُ **أَكْبَرْنَهُ** وَقَطَّعْنَ أَيْدِيَهُنَّ | When they saw him, they were astonished by him and cut their hands | Women of Egypt seeing Yūsuf — overwhelmed by his beauty |

---

### بَاب ٤ — Form V: تَكَبَّرَ (takabbara) — "to act great / be arrogant"

**Pattern: تَفَعَّلَ — prefix تَ + doubled middle letter.**

Form V is the reflexive of Form II. If كَبَّرَ (Form II) means "to declare something great," then تَكَبَّرَ (Form V) means "to declare *oneself* great" — to act arrogant, to carry oneself with false pride.

When Allah is described as **الْمُتَكَبِّرُ**, it is beautiful — He truly *is* the Supreme. When a human is called **مُتَكَبِّر**, it is a warning.

**Verb: 2 occurrences. Active participle (مُتَكَبِّر): 7 occurrences.**

### بَاب ٥ — Form X: اسْتَكْبَرَ (istakbara) — "to seek greatness / refuse out of pride"

**Pattern: اسْتَفْعَلَ — prefix اسْتَ.**

Form X often means to seek or claim the quality of the root for oneself. So اسْتَكْبَرَ means "to seek greatness for oneself" — to puff up, to refuse out of pride. This is the **most frequent** form of ك ب ر in the Qur'an. It echoes through every story of rejection: Iblīs, Pharaoh, the people of Nūḥ, ʿĀd, Thamūd, and every nation that rejected their prophet.

**Verb: 40 occurrences. Active participle (مُسْتَكْبِر): 6 occurrences. Verbal noun (اسْتِكْبَار): 2 occurrences.**

## Hadith & Dua Connections

### Hadith

**1. The Key to Prayer — Takbīrat al-Iḥrām**

> مِفْتَاحُ الصَّلَاةِ الطُّهُورُ، وَتَحْرِيمُهَا التَّكْبِيرُ، وَتَحْلِيلُهَا التَّسْلِيمُ

> *Miftāḥu ṣ-ṣalāti ṭ-ṭuhūr, wa taḥrīmuhā t-takbīr, wa taḥlīluhā t-taslīm.*

> "The key to prayer is purification, its opening is the takbīr, and its closing is the taslīm."

> — Sunan Abī Dāwūd, Sunan at-Tirmidhī

**2. Words Beloved to Allah**

> أَحَبُّ الْكَلَامِ إِلَى اللَّهِ أَرْبَعٌ: سُبْحَانَ اللَّهِ، وَالْحَمْدُ لِلَّهِ، وَلَا إِلَهَ إِلَّا اللَّهُ، وَاللهُ أَكْبَرُ

> *Aḥabbu l-kalāmi ila Allāhi arba': SubḥānAllāh, wal-ḥamdu lillāh, wa lā ilāha illa Allāh, wa Allāhu Akbar.*

> "The most beloved words to Allah are four: SubḥānAllāh, Alḥamdulillāh, Lā ilāha illallāh, and Allāhu Akbar."

> — Ṣaḥīḥ Muslim

**3. Sins Washed Like Sea Foam**

> مَنْ سَبَّحَ اللهَ فِي دُبُرِ كُلِّ صَلَاةٍ ثَلَاثًا وَثَلَاثِينَ، وَحَمِدَ اللهَ ثَلَاثًا وَثَلَاثِينَ، وَكَبَّرَ اللهَ أَرْبَعًا وَثَلَاثِينَ...

> *Man sabbaḥa Allāha fī duburi kulli ṣalātin thalāthan wa thalāthīn, wa ḥamida Allāha thalāthan wa thalāthīn, wa kabbara Allāha arba'an wa thalāthīn...*

> "Whoever glorifies Allah 33 times after every prayer, praises Allah 33 times, and declares Allah's greatness 34 times — then says 'Lā ilāha illallāhu waḥdahu lā sharīka lahu...' — his sins will be forgiven even if they were like the foam of the sea."

> — Ṣaḥīḥ Muslim

**4. The Danger of Kibr — An Atom's Weight**

> لَا يَدْخُلُ الْجَنَّةَ مَنْ كَانَ فِي قَلْبِهِ مِثْقَالُ ذَرَّةٍ مِنْ كِبْرٍ

> *Lā yadkhulu l-jannata man kāna fī qalbihi mithqāla dharratin min kibr.*

> "No one who has an atom's weight of kibr (arrogance) in his heart will enter Paradise."

> A man asked: "What if a person likes to have nice clothes and shoes?" The Prophet ﷺ said:

> إِنَّ اللهَ جَمِيلٌ يُحِبُّ الْجَمَالَ. الْكِبْرُ بَطَرُ الْحَقِّ وَغَمْطُ النَّاسِ

> *Inna Allāha jamīlun yuḥibbu l-jamāl. Al-kibru: baṭaru l-ḥaqqi wa ghamṭu n-nās.*

> "Allah is beautiful and loves beauty. Kibr is rejecting the truth and looking down on people."

> — Ṣaḥīḥ Muslim

**5. Kibr is Allah's Cloak (Ḥadīth Qudsī)**

> الْكِبْرِيَاءُ رِدَائِي، وَالْعَظَمَةُ إِزَارِي، فَمَنْ نَازَعَنِي وَاحِدًا مِنْهُمَا قَذَفْتُهُ فِي النَّارِ

> *Al-kibriyā'u ridā'ī, wa l-'aẓamatu izārī, fa man nāza'anī wāḥidan minhumā qadhaftuhu fi n-nār.*

> "Majesty (kibriyā') is My cloak and greatness is My garment. Whoever disputes with Me over one of them — I will cast him into the Fire."

> — Ṣaḥīḥ Muslim, Sunan Abī Dāwūd

**6. Twelve Angels Rushed — The Beautiful Takbīr**

> A man began prayer behind the Prophet ﷺ and said:

> اللهُ أَكْبَرُ كَبِيرًا، وَالْحَمْدُ لِلَّهِ كَثِيرًا، وَسُبْحَانَ اللهِ بُكْرَةً وَأَصِيلًا

> *Allāhu akbaru kabīrā, wa l-ḥamdu lillāhi kathīrā, wa subḥānAllāhi bukratan wa aṣīlā.*

> "Allah is incomparably great, much praise is for Allah, and glory to Allah in the morning and evening."

> The Prophet ﷺ said: "I was amazed — the gates of heaven were opened for those words."

> — Ṣaḥīḥ Muslim

**7. Umar's Takbīr in Mina**

> عُمَرُ كَانَ يُكَبِّرُ فِي قُبَّتِهِ بِمِنًى فَيَسْمَعُهُ أَهْلُ الْمَسْجِدِ فَيُكَبِّرُونَ وَيُكَبِّرُ أَهْلُ الْأَسْوَاقِ حَتَّى تَرْتَجَّ مِنًى تَكْبِيرًا

> *'Umar kāna yukabbiru fī qubbatihi bi-Minan, fa yasma'uhu ahlu l-masjid fa yukabbirūn, wa yukabbiru ahlu l-aswāq, ḥattā tartajja Minan takbīrā.*

> "Umar used to make takbīr in his tent in Mina, and the people of the masjid would hear it and make takbīr, and then the people of the marketplace would make takbīr, until all of Mina echoed with takbīr."

> — Ṣaḥīḥ al-Bukhārī (mu'allaq)

### Duas

**1. Post-Prayer Tasbīḥ (Daily)**

> سُبْحَانَ اللهِ (×33) — الْحَمْدُ لِلَّهِ (×33) — **اللهُ أَكْبَرُ** (×34)

> *SubḥānAllāh (×33) — Alḥamdulillāh (×33) — **Allāhu Akbar** (×34)*

> Then: لَا إِلَٰهَ إِلَّا اللهُ وَحْدَهُ لَا شَرِيكَ لَهُ، لَهُ الْمُلْكُ وَلَهُ الْحَمْدُ وَهُوَ عَلَىٰ كُلِّ شَيْءٍ قَدِيرٌ

> — Ṣaḥīḥ Muslim

**2. Opening of Prayer (Istiftāḥ)**

> اللهُ أَكْبَرُ كَبِيرًا، وَالْحَمْدُ لِلَّهِ كَثِيرًا، وَسُبْحَانَ اللهِ بُكْرَةً وَأَصِيلًا

> *Allāhu akbaru kabīrā, wal-ḥamdu lillāhi kathīrā, wa subḥānAllāhi bukratan wa aṣīlā.*

> "Allah is incomparably great, much praise is for Allah, and glory to Allah in the morning and evening."

> — Ṣaḥīḥ Muslim

**3. Takbīrāt al-'Eid (Eid Dhikr)**

> اللهُ أَكْبَرُ اللهُ أَكْبَرُ، لَا إِلَٰهَ إِلَّا اللهُ، وَاللهُ أَكْبَرُ اللهُ أَكْبَرُ وَلِلَّهِ الْحَمْدُ

> *Allāhu akbar, Allāhu akbar, lā ilāha illAllāh, wa Allāhu akbar, Allāhu akbar, wa lillāhi l-ḥamd.*

> "Allah is Greater, Allah is Greater, there is no god but Allah, and Allah is Greater, Allah is Greater, and to Allah belongs all praise."

> — Well-known practice from the Companions

**4. Dhikr Before Sleep**

> سُبْحَانَ اللهِ (×33) — الْحَمْدُ لِلَّهِ (×33) — **اللهُ أَكْبَرُ** (×34)

> Fāṭimah asked the Prophet ﷺ for a servant. He taught her this dhikr before sleep instead and said: "This is better for you than a servant."

> — Ṣaḥīḥ al-Bukhārī, Ṣaḥīḥ Muslim

We began with **اللهُ أَكْبَرُ**. Now you have heard this root — ك ب ر — move through the entire Qur'an.

You heard it in **Allah's names**: al-Kabīr, al-Mutakabbir.
You heard it in **commands to you**: فَكَبِّرْ — "Magnify Him." لِتُكَبِّرُوا — "So that you magnify Allah."
You heard it as a **warning**: اسْتَكْبَرَ — when Iblīs puffed himself up. When Pharaoh refused.
You heard it describe **old age**: كِبَر — Ibrāhīm, Zakariyyā, blessed with children when they were old.
You heard it describe **great sins**: كَبَائِر — the things we ask Allah to help us avoid.
And you heard it in the most **beautiful dua**: اللهُ أَكْبَرُ كَبِيرًا — the words that made the gates of heaven fly open.

**Teacher cue:** "Every time you raise your hands and say 'Allāhu Akbar,' you are using the same root that runs through all of this. *You* are doing takbīr. *You* are declaring that Allah is greater — greater than your worries, greater than this world, greater than anything your heart can imagine. Now when you hear it in the Qur'an, you'll recognize it like a familiar voice."

**Students should feel:**
- The root ك ب ر is everywhere — they will start noticing it in recitation
- "Allāhu Akbar" is not a slogan — it is connected to an entire world of meaning in the Qur'an
- The contrast between Allah's beautiful greatness and human arrogance is the root's central tension
- They already say this root every single day — now they *feel* what it means
