## Anchor
{: #anchor}

**أَشْهَدُ أَنْ لَا إِلَٰهَ إِلَّا ٱللَّهُ** — *I bear witness that there is no god but Allah*

<p class="audio-label">🔊 Adhān · أَشْهَدُ أَنْ لَا إِلَٰهَ إِلَّا ٱللَّهُ</p>

<div class="lang-en" markdown="1">
Every Muslim says this at least nine times a day — in every adhān and iqāmah. The word **أَشْهَدُ** is the same root as the witness in a courtroom, a prophet speaking to Allah on Judgement Day, body parts testifying on your behalf. When you say this, you are giving your personal testimony — *I, personally, bear witness.*

## Root word: شَهِدَ (to witness, to testify)
{: #root-shahida}

<div class="lang-en" markdown="1">

The root **ش ه د** (shin hā dāl) carries one core idea: *to be present at something and attest to it.* Whether that is a legal witness in court, a prophet testifying before Allah, or a martyr who witnesses his faith with his life — they all come from this root.

When you say **أَشْهَدُ** in the adhān, you are not reciting a formula. You are taking the stand as a witness.

- **شَهِدَ** is the base verb — *testified, bore witness*
- **شَهَادَة** is the noun — *testimony*. Also the name of the declaration of faith itself.
- **شَهِيد** is the one who witnesses — *a witness*. Also *a martyr*, because a martyr witnesses faith with their life.
- **شَاهِد** is one who witnesses — *an eyewitness*, present at the scene.

</div>

<div class="lang-ta" markdown="1">
</div>

### ⭐ · 1 Anchor Phrase

أَشْهَدُ أَنْ لَا **إِلَٰهَ** إِلَّا ٱللَّهُ

🔊 Adhān · <audio class="lang-en" controls preload="none" src="{{ '/assets/audio/lessons/lesson-02/anchor-shahida.mp3' | relative_url }}"></audio><audio class="lang-ta" controls preload="none" src="{{ '/assets/audio/lessons/lesson-02/anchor-shahida-ta.mp3' | relative_url }}"></audio>

"I bear witness that there is no **god** but Allah"

The phrase you carry into every prayer. Now you know the root behind أَشْهَدُ — and you already know لَا إِلَٰهَ إِلَّا from Lesson 1.

---

### 2 · شَهِدَ (testified)

**شَهِدَ** ٱللَّهُ أَنَّهُ لَا إِلَٰهَ إِلَّا هُوَ

(Āl ʿImrān 3:18) · <audio class="lang-en" controls preload="none" src="{{ '/assets/audio/lessons/lesson-02/learn-shahida-01.mp3' | relative_url }}"></audio><audio class="lang-ta" controls preload="none" src="{{ '/assets/audio/lessons/lesson-02/learn-shahida-01-ta.mp3' | relative_url }}"></audio>

"**Allah bears witness** that there is no god but He"

The same verb as أَشْهَدُ in the anchor — but the subject is Allah Himself. And what He is witnessing: لَا إِلَٰهَ إِلَّا هُوَ — the exact phrase from Lesson 1's anchor.

---

### 3 · شَهَادَة (testimony)

قُلْ أَيُّ شَيْءٍ أَكْبَرُ **شَهَادَةً** ۖ قُلِ اللَّهُ **شَهِيدٌ** بَيْنِي وَبَيْنَكُمْ

(Al-Anʿām 6:19) · <audio class="lang-en" controls preload="none" src="{{ '/assets/audio/lessons/lesson-02/learn-shahida-02.mp3' | relative_url }}"></audio><audio class="lang-ta" controls preload="none" src="{{ '/assets/audio/lessons/lesson-02/learn-shahida-02-ta.mp3' | relative_url }}"></audio>

"Say: What is greatest as **testimony**? Say: Allah is **Witness** between me and you"

Two forms in one verse — **شَهَادَة** (testimony) and **شَهِيد** (the Witness). And right there in the same verse: **أَكْبَرُ** — your word from Lesson 1.

---

### 4 · شَهِيد (witness)

وَكَفَىٰ بِاللَّهِ **شَهِيدًا**

(An-Nisāʾ 4:79) · <audio class="lang-en" controls preload="none" src="{{ '/assets/audio/lessons/lesson-02/learn-shahida-03.mp3' | relative_url }}"></audio><audio class="lang-ta" controls preload="none" src="{{ '/assets/audio/lessons/lesson-02/learn-shahida-03-ta.mp3' | relative_url }}"></audio>

"Allah is sufficient as **Witness**"

Three words. This exact phrase appears four times in the Quran — a recurring refrain. **شَهِيدٌ** in its most concentrated form.

---

### 5 · شَهِيد (personal witness)

وَكُنتُ عَلَيْهِمْ **شَهِيدًا** مَّا دُمْتُ فِيهِمْ

(Al-Māʾidah 5:117) · <audio class="lang-en" controls preload="none" src="{{ '/assets/audio/lessons/lesson-02/learn-shahida-04.mp3' | relative_url }}"></audio><audio class="lang-ta" controls preload="none" src="{{ '/assets/audio/lessons/lesson-02/learn-shahida-04-ta.mp3' | relative_url }}"></audio>

"And I was a **witness** over them while I was among them"

The same form **شَهِيدًا** — but first person. This is ʿĪsā speaking to Allah on the Day of Judgment. Not "Allah is Witness" but "I, personally, was a witness."

---

### 6 · شَهِدَ (present during)

فَمَن **شَهِدَ** مِنكُمُ الشَّهْرَ فَلْيَصُمْهُ

(Al-Baqarah 2:185) · <audio class="lang-en" controls preload="none" src="{{ '/assets/audio/lessons/lesson-02/learn-shahida-05.mp3' | relative_url }}"></audio><audio class="lang-ta" controls preload="none" src="{{ '/assets/audio/lessons/lesson-02/learn-shahida-05-ta.mp3' | relative_url }}"></audio>

"Whoever **witnesses** the month (of Ramadan) should fast it"

The base verb again — but now شَهِدَ doesn't mean "testify in court." It means *to be present during, to live through.* You witness the month by being there for it. The root stretches.

### 7 · الشَّاهِدِينَ (the witnesses)

رَبَّنَا آمَنَّا بِمَا أَنزَلْتَ وَاتَّبَعْنَا الرَّسُولَ فَاكْتُبْنَا مَعَ **الشَّاهِدِينَ**

(Āl ʿImrān 3:53) · <audio class="lang-en" controls preload="none" src="{{ '/assets/audio/lessons/lesson-02/practice-01.mp3' | relative_url }}"></audio><audio class="lang-ta" controls preload="none" src="{{ '/assets/audio/lessons/lesson-02/practice-01-ta.mp3' | relative_url }}"></audio>

"Our Lord, we believe in what You sent down and followed the Messenger — write us among **the witnesses**"

The duʿāʾ of ʿĪsā's disciples. **الشَّاهِدِينَ** — the witnesses, plural. They asked to be counted among this company.

---

### 8 · نَشْهَدُ + يَشْهَدُ (we testify + He testifies)

قَالُوا **نَشْهَدُ** إِنَّكَ لَرَسُولُ اللَّهِ وَاللَّهُ **يَشْهَدُ** إِنَّ الْمُنَافِقِينَ لَكَاذِبُونَ

(Al-Munāfiqūn 63:1) · <audio class="lang-en" controls preload="none" src="{{ '/assets/audio/lessons/lesson-02/practice-02.mp3' | relative_url }}"></audio><audio class="lang-ta" controls preload="none" src="{{ '/assets/audio/lessons/lesson-02/practice-02-ta.mp3' | relative_url }}"></audio>

"They say: **We testify** you are Allah's Messenger — but Allah **testifies** that the hypocrites are liars"

Two verb forms back to back — **نَشْهَدُ** (we testify) vs **يَشْهَدُ** (He testifies). The hypocrites claim the same root, but Allah overrules their testimony with His own.

---

### 9 · شَهِدَ (body parts testify)

**شَهِدَ** عَلَيْهِمْ سَمْعُهُمْ وَأَبْصَارُهُمْ وَجُلُودُهُم بِمَا كَانُوا يَعْمَلُونَ

(Fuṣṣilat 41:20) · <audio class="lang-en" controls preload="none" src="{{ '/assets/audio/lessons/lesson-02/practice-03.mp3' | relative_url }}"></audio><audio class="lang-ta" controls preload="none" src="{{ '/assets/audio/lessons/lesson-02/practice-03-ta.mp3' | relative_url }}"></audio>

"Their hearing, their sight, and their skins **testified** against them for what they used to do"

On the Day of Judgment, the subject of **شَهِدَ** is not a person — it is their own body parts. Their hearing, sight, and skin take the stand against them.

---

### 10 · اشْهَدُوا (bear witness — command)

قَالَ فَ**اشْهَدُوا** وَأَنَا مَعَكُم مِّنَ **الشَّاهِدِينَ**

(Āl ʿImrān 3:81) · <audio class="lang-en" controls preload="none" src="{{ '/assets/audio/lessons/lesson-02/practice-04.mp3' | relative_url }}"></audio><audio class="lang-ta" controls preload="none" src="{{ '/assets/audio/lessons/lesson-02/practice-04-ta.mp3' | relative_url }}"></audio>

"He said: Then **bear witness** — and I am with you among **the witnesses**"

Two forms back to back — the imperative **اشْهَدُوا** (you all, bear witness!) and the plural noun **الشَّاهِدِينَ**. Allah gives the command, then joins as a witness Himself.

---

### 11 · الشُّهَدَاء (the witnesses — highest rank)

مَعَ الَّذِينَ أَنْعَمَ اللَّهُ عَلَيْهِم مِّنَ النَّبِيِّينَ وَالصِّدِّيقِينَ وَ**الشُّهَدَاءِ** وَالصَّالِحِينَ

(An-Nisāʾ 4:69) · <audio class="lang-en" controls preload="none" src="{{ '/assets/audio/lessons/lesson-02/practice-05.mp3' | relative_url }}"></audio><audio class="lang-ta" controls preload="none" src="{{ '/assets/audio/lessons/lesson-02/practice-05-ta.mp3' | relative_url }}"></audio>

"With those Allah has blessed — the prophets, the truthful, **the witnesses**, and the righteous"

**الشُّهَدَاء** — a different plural form. The four ranks of the blessed. Every Muslim prays to be in this company.

### 12 · اللَّهُمَّ (O Allah — recall)

قُلِ **اللَّهُمَّ** فَاطِرَ السَّمَاوَاتِ وَالْأَرْضِ عَالِمَ الْغَيْبِ وَالشَّهَادَةِ

(Az-Zumar 39:46) · <audio class="lang-en" controls preload="none" src="{{ '/assets/audio/lessons/lesson-02/recall-01.mp3' | relative_url }}"></audio><audio class="lang-ta" controls preload="none" src="{{ '/assets/audio/lessons/lesson-02/recall-01-ta.mp3' | relative_url }}"></audio>

"Say: O **Allah**, Creator of the heavens and earth, Knower of the unseen and the witnessed"

**اللَّهُمَّ** from Lesson 1 — and also الشَّهَادَة from today's root. Here الشَّهَادَة means *the seen world* — the opposite of الغَيْب (the unseen). Same root, different shade.

---

### 13 · آلِهَة + إِلَٰه (gods → god — recall)

أَجَعَلَ **الآلِهَةَ** **إِلَٰهًا** وَاحِدًا إِنَّ هَذَا لَشَيْءٌ عُجَابٌ

(Ṣād 38:5) · <audio class="lang-en" controls preload="none" src="{{ '/assets/audio/lessons/lesson-02/recall-02.mp3' | relative_url }}"></audio><audio class="lang-ta" controls preload="none" src="{{ '/assets/audio/lessons/lesson-02/recall-02-ta.mp3' | relative_url }}"></audio>

"Has he made the **gods** into one **God**?! This is truly astonishing!"

Both Lesson 1 forms in one phrase — **آلِهَة** (gods, plural) and **إِلَٰه** (god, singular) — side by side. Quraysh mocking the idea that all their gods could be one.

---

### 14 · أَكْبَرُ (greater — cosmic scale — recall)

لَخَلْقُ السَّمَاوَاتِ وَالْأَرْضِ **أَكْبَرُ** مِنْ خَلْقِ النَّاسِ

(Ghāfir 40:57) · <audio class="lang-en" controls preload="none" src="{{ '/assets/audio/lessons/lesson-02/recall-03.mp3' | relative_url }}"></audio><audio class="lang-ta" controls preload="none" src="{{ '/assets/audio/lessons/lesson-02/recall-03-ta.mp3' | relative_url }}"></audio>

"The creation of the heavens and earth is **greater** than the creation of mankind"

Your word from Lesson 1 — **أَكْبَرُ** — but now comparing creation at cosmic scale to human scale. Same form, a universe of difference.

---

### 15 · كَبِير (great — every great thing — recall)

وَكُلُّ صَغِيرٍ وَ**كَبِيرٍ** مُّسْتَطَرٌ

(Al-Qamar 54:53) · <audio class="lang-en" controls preload="none" src="{{ '/assets/audio/lessons/lesson-02/recall-04.mp3' | relative_url }}"></audio><audio class="lang-ta" controls preload="none" src="{{ '/assets/audio/lessons/lesson-02/recall-04-ta.mp3' | relative_url }}"></audio>

"Every small and **great** thing is recorded"

Four words from Surah Al-Qamar. **كَبِير** paired with its opposite **صَغِير** (small) — every great thing, without exception, is recorded.

---

### 16 · إِلَٰه (god — Allah to Mūsā — recall)

إِنَّنِي أَنَا اللَّهُ لَا **إِلَٰهَ** إِلَّا أَنَا فَاعْبُدْنِي وَأَقِمِ الصَّلَاةَ لِذِكْرِي

(Ṭā Hā 20:14) · <audio class="lang-en" controls preload="none" src="{{ '/assets/audio/lessons/lesson-02/recall-05.mp3' | relative_url }}"></audio><audio class="lang-ta" controls preload="none" src="{{ '/assets/audio/lessons/lesson-02/recall-05-ta.mp3' | relative_url }}"></audio>

"Indeed I am Allah — there is no **god** but Me, so worship Me and establish prayer for My remembrance"

Allah speaking directly to Mūsā at the burning bush. The same لَا **إِلَٰهَ** إِلَّا pattern from Lesson 1's anchor — but now in Allah's own voice.

## Closing
{: #closing}

<div class="lang-en" markdown="1">

This lesson moved through one question — *what does it mean to witness?* The answer came from many directions: Allah witnessing His own oneness, a prophet testifying at the end of his ministry, body parts taking the stand, hypocrites whose testimony Allah overturns.

And underneath all of it: the root you already carry every time you say **أَشْهَدُ أَنْ لَا إِلَٰهَ إِلَّا ٱللَّهُ**.

From now on, listen for this root. When you hear **شَهِيد** in the Quran — whether it means witness or martyr — you will know it comes from the same place as your declaration in the adhān. When the word **شَهَادَة** comes up in a verse, you will hear the noun behind the shahādah itself. That moment of recognition is the lesson working.

</div>
